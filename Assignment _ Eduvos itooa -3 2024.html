Study the scenario and complete the questions that follow:

 

Pay Day for Emily

It's pay day! Emily, because she has been paid, has decided to go and withdraw some money from an automated teller machine (ATM). The withdrawal transaction starts by Emily inserting her bank card into the ATM. The ATM validates the card, and if it's valid prompts Emily for his personal identity number (PIN). If the card is invalid / unreadable, the ATM ejects the inserted card after displaying an appropriate message on the screen. Once the PIN is entered, the information is sent to Emily's bank to validate whether the entered PIN is valid. If the PIN is invalid, the ATM ejects the inserted card after displaying an appropriate message on the screen without prompting Emily for another try. Emily has three attempts before the card is blocked.  If the PIN is valid, the ATM then prompts Emily for her desired amount. That information is again sent to Emily's bank to ascertain whether the requested amount is within her current balance. If the requested amount is more than the current balance, the ATM displays this information on the screen and ejects the inserted card without prompting Emily for another try. If the requested amount is within the current balance, the ATM dispenses the requested amount for Emily to take and the bank adjusts Emily's account accordingly. The latest account balance is then displayed on the ATM screen and the ATM then completes the transaction by ejecting Emily's card. Emily takes her card and runs off to go do some shopping.

Source: Zifunzi, A. 2023.

(10 Marks)

 

1.2     Based on the scenario, create a state diagram to show the possible states that a card can be in.

Question 4

Vehicle Insurance

A leading insurance company in South Africa is running a promotion for its current customers. They are giving a 30 per cent discount to a customer if the customer adds another vehicle to an already existing car insurance policy. The customer must call the insurance call centre and provide them with their policy number. The insurance clerk enters the policy number into their system and information about the customer’s basic insurance policy will be displayed.

The insurance clerk will then check if the policy is up to date. The customer will then provide them with the specifications of the car to be added (model, make, year and VIN). This information is entered and validated for authenticity. After it is authenticated, the customer will then choose the cover type and premium fee. The clerk enters this information and records it. The system then validates the total coverage against other ranges.

Lastly, the customer is prompted to present information about the other drivers of the customer’s cars. If they are a new driver, another use case called “Add new driver” is invoked. Eventually, the policy is updated and new premiums are calculated. The updated policy will be printed and sent to the policy owner.

Adapted Source: Dennis, A., Tegarden, D. and Wixom, B. 2021. Systems Analysis and Design: An Object-Oriented Approach with UML. 6th edition. USA: Wiley.
